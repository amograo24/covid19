Black Fungus


Black fungus/mucormycosis is a rare infection that can be life-threatening at times. It is caused by the mucor mould commonly found in soil, manure and decaying vegetables and fruits.

The infection becomes life threatening in immunosuppressed and in diabetic individuals. It has been on a rampant spread due to the overuse of steroids for treating COVID-19 patients. Steroids have been found to reduce inflammation in the lungs but at the same time, they suppress the immune system due to which, the patients become vulnerable to these infections. The overuse of steroids has been a trigger for mucormycosis infections.

Patients suffering from this fungal infection typically have sypmtoms such as:

  • Stuffy and bleeding nose
  • Swelling of and pain in the eye
  • Drooping of eyelids
  • Blurred and finally, loss of vision.
  • There could be black patches of skin around the nose
